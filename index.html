I am a Doctoral Researcher in the Department of Mathematics at Purdue University, mentored by Dr. Victor D. Lie.

My research interests encompass classical harmonic analysis and geometric measure theory.

In particular, emphasis is on time–frequency analysis and multilinear operators such as the Bilinear and Trilinear Hilbert Transforms and Carleson-type operators, together with their corresponding zero and non-zero curvature analogues.

A central objective of my work is to develop a unified perspective and set of tools for approaching both zero and non-zero curvature problems. A complementary direction of my research focuses on applications of these ideas to partial differential equations and geometric measure theory.
